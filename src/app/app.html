Survival Guide

Essential things to know before landing in Mexico City

Mexican Currency (MXN)

The local currency is the Mexican Peso (MXN), and it is the only accepted currency for everyday purchases. We highly recommend bringing US Dollars (USD) or Euros (EUR) from your home country, as they are the easiest and most favorable to exchange upon arrival.

Arrival at AICM

Uber & ride-sharing pick-ups are NOT permitted at AICM terminals for either departures or arrivals.

Use the authorized airport taxis located inside the terminal buildings. Purchase your ticket at the taxi booth before exiting.

Tip: Once you leave the airport area, Uber works normally across the city.

Getting Around

Mexico City's Metro is one of the largest in the world — and incredibly affordable

1

Get a MI Card

Purchase a Movilidad Integrada (MI) card at any Metro station. It costs ~$21 MXN and works for Metro, Metrobús, Cablebús & Trolley.

2

Recharge

Add credit at automated machines inside stations using cash or bank cards. Each Metro trip costs $5 MXN.

3

Ride

Tap your card at the turnstile and enjoy! The Metro operates from 5:00 AM to midnight.

We recommend using Google Maps always for navigating the city
Weather Alert: Rain is expected during the event dates. Don't forget to pack an umbrella and a light jacket!

Places to Explore

Must-visit destinations in Mexico City — with Metro directions from DFIE

At night, prefer Uber over Metro for safety and convenience. The Metro closes at midnight.

Zócalo & Historic Center

The beating heart of Mexico City — the main square, the Cathedral, and the National Palace.

Lindavista → transfer at Deportivo 18 de Marzo → Zócalo Station (Line 2)

Chapultepec Castle & Park

A massive urban park with a castle, museums, and a lake. One of the world's largest city parks.

Lindavista → Dep. 18 de Marzo → Chapultepec Station (Line 1)

Palacio de Bellas Artes

Stunning Art Nouveau palace featuring murals by Diego Rivera and stunning architecture.

Lindavista → Dep. 18 de Marzo → Bellas Artes Station (Line 2/8)

Coyoacán & Frida Kahlo Museum

Bohemian neighborhood home to the iconic Frida Kahlo "Casa Azul" museum and beautiful colonial streets.

Lindavista → transfer → Coyoacán Station (Line 3) + walk

Paseo de la Reforma & Ángel de la Independencia

The city's most famous avenue with monuments, skyscrapers, and the iconic golden Angel.

Lindavista → Dep. 18 de Marzo → Insurgentes Station (Line 1) + walk

Monumento a la Revolución

A massive arch commemorating the Mexican Revolution, featuring a spectacular observation deck.

Lindavista → Dep. 18 de Marzo → Revolución Station (Line 2)

Plaza Antara (Polanco)

An upscale open-air shopping mall in Polanco with premium boutiques, restaurants, and nightlife.

Lindavista → El Rosario → Polanco Station (Line 7) + Uber

Reforma 222

A striking, modern architectural complex housing a stylish mall right on Paseo de la Reforma.

Lindavista → Dep. 18 de Marzo → Hidalgo Station (Line 3) + Walk

Parque Lindavista

The nearest large shopping center right in our district, offering great dining, shops, and cinemas.

Walking distance! (~5 minutes from IPN)
